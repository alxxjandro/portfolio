# .impeccable.md — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, potential clients, and fellow developers landing on the portfolio from a link or search. They're scanning quickly on desktop — forming an impression in under 10 seconds — and then digging deeper into specific projects. The goal is to pass a "sharp and capable" filter fast, then reward curiosity with depth.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** minimal · precise · sharp

Voice: direct and self-assured, never salesy or inflated. Alonso speaks as someone who ships real things — not someone performing the idea of a developer. The portfolio should feel like it was made by a person, not generated.

Emotional goal: leave visitors feeling "this person is creative and experimental, *and* gets things done" — the rare combination.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Understated and content-first. Let the work speak; don't dress up the container. Inspired by [sonnet.io](https://sonnet.io/) (personality through restraint, no spectacle) and [huyml.co](https://huyml.co/) (modular hierarchy, purposeful whitespace, professional-grade craft).
- **Color:** Green `#22c55e` is the single accent — used sparingly as a signal, not wallpaper. Background is white (light) or `#1f2937` (dark). No additional hues.
- **Typography:** Roboto for body, Roboto Mono for code. Type should feel intentional — size jumps, weight contrast, and spacing do the heavy lifting.
- **Theme:** Both light and dark modes are supported. Desktop is the primary experience; mobile should be clean but is not the priority.
- **Anti-references:** No mesh gradients, glassmorphism, generic blob shapes, or anything that reads as "AI-generated UI kit." No purple-to-pink gradients. No floating cards with drop shadows stacked four layers deep. No hero sections with a centered gradient orb.

### Design Principles

1. **Every pixel earns its place.** If an element doesn't communicate something, remove it. Decoration that doesn't reinforce meaning is noise.

2. **Sharp over safe.** Prefer confident, decisive choices — a bold type size, a tight spacing rhythm, a single strong accent — over hedged, "balanced" mediocrity.

3. **Human-made, not generated.** Specificity is the antidote to the AI aesthetic. Use real screenshots, real project names, real copy. Avoid pattern-library defaults and generic UI tropes.

4. **Desktop-first, mobile-acceptable.** Design and optimize for the full-screen experience. Mobile adaptations should be clean and functional but never at the cost of the desktop composition.

5. **Motion serves interaction, not attention.** Animations should confirm an action or guide the eye — not perform. Subtle, fast transitions (100–200ms). No loops unless they communicate something (the hero card's rotation is an exception because it's thematic).
